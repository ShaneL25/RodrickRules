Diary of a Wimpy Kid: Rodrick Rules

By Jeff Kinney

Summary

*Diary of a Wimpy Kid: Rodrick Rules* is the second book in the Wimpy Kid series. The story is told from first person by Greg Heffley, who is in middle school and lives with his mom, dad, older brother Rodrick, and younger brother manny. He also has his best friend rowley jefferson.

Over the summer, Greg dealt with embarrassment and family tension. As the school year starts, Greg tries to avoid trouble but often gets dragged in by Rodrick. Their mom begins a “Mom Bucks” program to encourage them to behave and get along. Rodrick has a science project due, but he doesn’t want to do it himself and tries to get Greg and others to help.

When Rodrick fakes being sick and their parents leave town, he throws a wild party at home and locks Greg in the basement to keep him from telling. Greg sneaks photos of the party as leverage. Their mother later discovers evidence of what happened, and both brothers face punishments. The book ends with Greg deciding to help Rodrick with his science project so Rodrick won’t fail and Greg won't be disgraced by the party scandal.

Main Characters

  • Greg Heffley — the narrator, a middle school kid who often tries to find shortcuts.
  • Rodrick Heffley — Greg’s older brother, likes to tease Greg and avoids responsibility.
  • Mom (Susan) & Dad (Frank) Heffley — they both try to keep peace and teach lessons at home.
  • Manny Heffley — Greg’s younger brother, often gets away with things.
  • Rowley Jefferson — Greg’s best friend, more innocent and loyal.

Lessons

A lesson in this book is that siblings will always respect each other even if on bad terms.

  • Siblings & Family Conflict: Greg and Rodrick have constant fights, but by the end, they realize they need each other.
  • Responsibility: Rodrick’s refusal to do his own work leads to consequences.
  • Empathy & Understanding: Though the brothers often clash, Greg tries to help Rodrick in the end so things don’t get worse for both.
